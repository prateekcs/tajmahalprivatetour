Delhi

Delhi, the vibrant capital of India is a fascinating blend of history and modernity, providing a fascinating visitor experience. Immersed in a rich fabric of culture and heritage, Delhi boasts of architectural marvels spanning the ages. A UNESCO World Heritage Site, the iconic Red Fort reminds us of India’s Mughal past as a great monument. The Qutub-Minar, is an ancient Islamic tower, that display its culture in its sculpture. History buffs will know that Delhi is a treasure trove of enchanting places that tell stories of past empires. Humayun’s tomb is a reflection of a bygone era, as is the grand Jama Masjid, India’s largest mosque.

Wander the bustling markets such as chaotic Chandni Chowk for a sensory overload. Bazaars overflow with sights, sounds and flavors - the perfect place to indulge in local delicacies or shop for traditional handicrafts. Memories of rulers from the past blend with the vibrant present in this fascinating capital city.

Chandni Chowk and other busy markets in the city provide an explosion of sensory experiences with sights, sounds, and tastes which allude to local food and old traditional crafts. Modern Delhi is also inviting through its modern shopping centres, active nightlife and blossoming art scene as well as culinary outlets that attract a wide range of people. The Bahá'í House of Worship, called Lotus Temple, epitomizes inclusivity and architectural advancement in Delhi. Delhi on its own is already adventure with its busy streets while offering efficient metro networks. It gives you a chance to visit ancient monuments , enjoy street food and feel close to warm hearts . In summary , it will be a trip like no other across Indian history from past up- to the present time.
